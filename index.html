Artist statement

I make images that feel like overheard transmissions from another future.

My work is interested in tension: soft human memory inside hard synthetic spaces, quiet moments surrounded by aggressive light, and the beauty of things built to outlast the people moving through them.

I use cyberpunk aesthetics not just for style, but as a way to talk about isolation, surveillance, speed, intimacy, and the strange romance of cities that never fully sleep.

Process

How each piece comes online.

01

Signal capture

I collect references from trains, street signage, industrial textures, films, and late-night photos.

02

Structure pass

Rough composition starts in grayscale or quick 3D blockouts to lock in mood and perspective.

03

Neon calibration

Color is layered carefully so the glow feels earned rather than sprayed across every surface.

04

Final distortion

Texture, grain, bloom, and selective glitch treatments give each piece its final pulse.

Core idea

"I want the work to feel cinematic, haunted, and strangely tender at the edge of collapse."
